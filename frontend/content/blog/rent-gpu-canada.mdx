## Why Rent a GPU in Canada?

If you're training machine learning models, running inference, or processing large datasets, buying your own GPU hardware is expensive and hard to maintain. Renting GPU time by the hour is the standard approach — but for Canadian teams, **where** you rent matters just as much as **what** you rent.

Three reasons jurisdiction matters when you rent a GPU in Canada:

- **PIPEDA compliance** — Canada's federal privacy law requires that personal data handled by Canadian businesses stays under Canadian legal jurisdiction. A US-controlled cloud provider operating a Canadian region doesn't satisfy this.
- **CLOUD Act exposure** — US-headquartered providers (AWS, Azure, GCP) can be compelled to hand over data stored anywhere in the world, including their Canadian regions.
- **Latency and data transfer** — If your datasets are already in Canada, keeping compute local avoids cross-border transfer costs and latency.

## Your Options for Renting GPUs in Canada

### 1. Hyperscaler "Canada Regions"

AWS (ca-central-1), Azure (Canada Central/East), and GCP (northamerica-northeast1) all offer GPU instances in Canadian data centres. They're reliable and well-documented, but:

- You're still under US jurisdiction via the CLOUD Act
- Pricing is in USD with markup compared to US regions
- Availability for high-end GPUs (H100, A100) is often limited
- Reserved instances require 1–3 year commitments

### 2. Canadian Bare-Metal Providers

Companies like OVHcloud (Beauharnois, QC) offer bare-metal GPU servers. You get dedicated hardware, but:

- No elasticity — you're paying whether or not you're computing
- Limited GPU selection (often previous-gen cards)
- You manage drivers, CUDA, containers, and networking yourself
