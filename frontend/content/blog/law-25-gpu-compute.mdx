## What Is Law 25?

Quebec's **Act to Modernize Legislative Provisions as Regards the Protection of Personal Information** — commonly called **Law 25** — came into full force on September 22, 2024. It's the most comprehensive privacy law in Canada, and in some ways, the strictest in North America.

For companies processing personal data of Quebec residents, Law 25 imposes obligations that go well beyond federal PIPEDA requirements:

- **Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs)** are mandatory before transferring data outside Quebec or using it for AI/ML training.
- **Express consent** is required for profiling, automated decision-making, and cross-border transfers.
- **Data portability** — individuals can demand their data in a structured, machine-readable format.
- **Breach notification** within 72 hours to the CAI (Commission d'accès à l'information).
- **Administrative penalties** up to $25 million or 4% of global turnover.

## The Compute Problem

If you're training a model on data that includes Quebec residents — patient records, financial transactions, customer interactions — the compute infrastructure itself falls under Law 25 scrutiny.

Specifically, Section 17 requires a **Privacy Impact Assessment** before any transfer of personal information outside Quebec. Using a US-headquartered cloud provider counts as a cross-border transfer, regardless of where the physical servers sit — because the US CLOUD Act gives foreign legal authority over that data.

This creates a compliance trap: you can use `ca-central-1` in Montreal and still trigger Law 25's PIA requirements because the data processor (AWS, Google, Azure) is controlled by a foreign entity.
